# Brand Direction

## Style Goal

The UI should feel like a modern enterprise POS platform for multi-location retail and service operations. It should be desktop-first, touch-friendly, clean, adaptable, and professional.

The design should work for multiple business types, including supermarket, pharmacy, electronics, general retail, and future restaurant/cafe workflows. It should not visually depend on one industry.

## Layout Direction

- Desktop-first POS terminal interface.
- Touch-friendly buttons and controls.
- Large readable text.
- Clear totals and payment states.
- Strong primary action buttons.
- Left sidebar navigation.
- Top status bar with Company, Location, Terminal, employee, shift status, and business date.
- Light main content area.
- Dense but readable checkout, management, and reporting layouts.
- Adaptable catalog area that can show Items for different business types.

## UI Personality

- Efficient.
- Trustworthy.
- Operational.
- Enterprise-ready.
- Calm under pressure.
- Easy to scan during high-volume checkout or service counter work.

## Color Guidance

- Primary: blue or dark navy.
- Success/payment: green.
- Danger/remove/void: red.
- Warning/manager approval: amber.
- Neutral background: light gray and white.

Final colors can be adjusted in Figma after the first prototype is generated.

## Component Guidance

- Use large numeric totals for Order amount, cash received, and change.
- Use clear Item rows in the cart.
- Use strong buttons for checkout, payment, shift, and cash actions.
- Use distinct visual states for pending, completed, verified, cancelled, approved, rejected, warning, and error.
- Use modal dialogs for manager approval and void/remove Item confirmation.
- Use dashboard cards only for repeated metrics and reports, not as decorative page wrappers.
- Keep icons and labels generic enough for multiple industries.
